inchoative |inˈkō-itiv|

Inchoate is an adjective that means just begun, not fully formed. An inchoative verb shows the process of beginning and becoming. It is the space between grow and grown.
It is growing.
It is development.

In latin, when you see -sc- in the midst of a word, it is an inchoative verb.

  • rubeo means to be red
  • rubescō means to grow red
    •

    The -sc- expands inside the word, even while the concept that the word represents is expanding. There is something exciting about that spark of action inside.
    It never ends. It always exists in the exact moment of action.
    Inchoatives iterate.
