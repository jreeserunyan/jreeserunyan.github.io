Enumerables

Keep counting

Sept 21, 2015

Looking at the word itself gives you a couple clues. It’s pretty obvious that it’s about counting over and over again. But let’s get deeper.

Enumerable methods “traverse, search, sort and manipulate collections”.

They have a lot in common with arrays and hashes, but they allow you to iterate over your collections with fewer lines of code. They are remarkable tools for organizing data. Since most of that is probably nonsense, let us work with an example.

When you use enumerable to map an array, you transform each element in the array, without changing the actual array.

Map is a simple, but very effective enumerable. Just like in a method, the last line in the code block is the value that gets transformed!
